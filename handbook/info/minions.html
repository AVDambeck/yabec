minions

Minions are NPCs including human and demihumans hired to assist on an adventure, trained animals, and even monsters.

Limit

Each character can be a boss to a finite number of minions, determined by Charisma.

Level

Minions must be lower level and have fewer hit dice than their boss

Item slots

A minion has 5 item slots. Some monsters, have different amounts of item slots.

Loyalty

Minions are not mindless slaves. If abused minions might desert, mutiny, betray, or slander their boss.

The starting loyalty of a hireling is indicated by charisma.

Monsters start with 4 loyalty.

Loyalty Check

Roll 2d6. The check succeeds if the result is equal to or less than the loyalty.

Peril

Dangerous tasks are perilous. A minion must succeed a loyalty check or refuse the task. Furthermore, if they take damage or come to any unexpected harm during the task, they loose one loyalty.

Evaluation

Minions evaluate loyalty during clean up.

Betrayal

Minions with one or less loyalty will betray their boss. Monsterous, and chaotic minions may cooperate only for a time, betraying their boss once the conditions are right.

Hirelings

­Hirelings are human or demihuman minions that seek employment from the party. 1d4 will approach a party with 2nd level or higher adventuerers.

Upkeep

Hirelings must be paid at the start of each session. They come with a starting wage. When a hireling levels up, their wage doubles.

Hirelings will accept a 50% reduction in wages in exchange for a half share of the treasure.

Once per session, wages can be reduced by 10% by reducing loyalty by one.

Items

The hiring PC must provide for food and lodging while traveling, as well as for any new adventuring gear, weapons, or mounts.

Stats

Hirelings have a d6 hit die. Their AV and Saves are on the progression table below. They can use any weapon, chain armor, leather armor, and shields.

Animals

Upkeep

Animals do not accept money or treasure. However they do need to be fed. Their upkeep cost is 1 cp per HD.

Recruitment

Some animals can be bought. See Gear. Wild animals can be tamed. See Downtime.

Monsters

Occasionally, monsters may be swayed to join the party. Monsters may demand food, treasure, a share of the treasure, or something else as payment.

Monsters with 10 or more loyalty will continue to serve their boss without upkeep. Each session this is done reduces their upkeep by one.
