Downtime

Returning characters can preform one downtime.

Second level characters of certain classes can preform advanced downtime

Community Service

Preforming service at the temple has no direct benifite, but may be required as penalty for crimes.

Except for the first time doing service, chaotic aligned characters can hire a goon to do service for them. The first time it costs 100 cp. Each time after costs 1% more, compounding. Diety disfavor can not be forgiven this way.

Worship

In order to worship, you must visit a temple matching your alignment. Each time you worship, you gain merit equal to you wisdom score plus 1d20. When you die, the temple will contribute cp to your funeral equal to 1/10th of your merit. There is also a chance they will be able to revive you, equal to merit/100 in 20

Additionally, divine spell-casters might be able to forgive diety disfavor. The chance is equal to their level, plus their wisdom, out of 100.

Baking

Help at the local bakery. Heal 1d6; if you are ar full HP, prevent the next 2 damage that would be dealt to you this session.

Healing

A stay in the temple of your alignment costs 100 cp, or one week of service. It completely heals you, cures mundane illnesses, has a 5-in-6 chance of curing mundane toxins, and a 2-in-6 chance of removing minor cureses.

Blessed Equipment

Loose any amount of XP upto the sum of all six ability scores. Add that much XP to an item. When that item has 333 XP, it becomes a +1 magical item. When it has 3,333, it becomes a +2 item.

Selling

Try to get a better buy on your treasure.

Each downtime, you can find buyers for treasure equal to 500 cp times your level. If a single object is more valuable than that, you must work for several downtimes to sell it.

When you sell the item, roll d% on the table, and add your charisma score.

If you get robbed, ask your Dungeon Master for a lead on the thieves.

Gambling

Play a number of games of Dragon Ante equal to thrice your level.

You might also hear rumors. Make two charisma checks. For each you succeed, ask the Dungeon Master for a rumor.

Challenging the red dragon

Roll a d12. On a 1, you must pay 50 cp, or preform a week of service. On a 2-11, you win 5 cp. On a 12, win 300 cp, or...

Choose to challenge Tiamat: Roll 6d6. Choose any number to reroll. Repeat this three more times. If you have six of a kind, or a 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6, you win 6 gp, 6 sp, and 6 cp.

Wild Wyvern

Once you 101 cp or more in a single week, you are invited to a Wild Wyvern table: an exclusive game for the best players. It is the same as dragon ante, with the following modifications:

  • Buy in, as well as the pay outs, and penalties are increased by a factor of 100.
  • instead of 2d10, use 4d6-1d12.
  • Whenever you roll two or more of a kind (except for five fives and four fours), you enter double dragon: wager an additional 500cp, reduce the number of games youll play by the number of a kind. Then add that many d4s to your roll.
  • When you roll five fives, you will 55555cp. When you roll four fours, you loose 4444cp.

Mentor

Finding a mentor

Training, employement, and some magical research requires a mentor. The target number is equal to:

  • 50
  • plus your charisma score
  • minus 10 times the level of the mentor you seek
  • plus however many additional sp you spend looking

Roll d%. If it is less than or equal to the target number, you found a mentor!

Make an NPC card for them, and add their level.

Employment

You can work form them. You can only work for a mentor of a higher level than you.

Your mentor pays you a number of cp equal to your Lv, times their Lv, times 20. Then make a Charisma Check. If you suceed, premanently increase your wage with this mentor by 20%.

Training

You can only train under a mentor of the same class and a higher level than you. traing costs 10 cp, times the level of your mentor.

Spend upto 100 times your Lv in cp. Gain that much experience. If you spend 20% more, your henchmen also gain half that much experience.

Team-building

Costs 50 cp per minion. Each participating minion has a 2-in-6 chance of gaining 1 loyalty, to a maximum of 8.

Taming

Mundane animals can be tamed by anyone. Druids, and Elves, can tame magical animals, and tame mundane animals twice as fast.

Each week, make two charisma, and two wisdom checks. (Druids and Elves make four of each). Once you gain a number of successes equal to 3 plus the HD of the animal, they becomes tamed. Then you can begin training it.

Each of the following skills costs a certain amount of successes. Once trained, the animal will automatically succeed at tasks related to the skill.

Questing

More adventuring!

Choose a challenge to face. Each round, you’ll make a melee attack against it, and then it will make a melee attack against you. You can flee at anytime.

Multipule characters can team up. Each character gets to make an attack, and the challange will make only one attack, chooseing a target at random.

If you slay the challenge, you gain its reward.

Fighters and Knights take half damage.
