Character Creation

Get a sheet of paper, or print out a character sheet, to record all of your cracter's details. Make sure to do so in pencil, as these details change over time. If your character sheet ever gets too messy, you can always copy the info to a fresh paper!

Note IRL Name and Date

Write your name, and todays date.

Ability Scores

Your character has six abilities:

  1. Strength
  2. Dexterity
  3. Constitution
  4. Intelligence
  5. Wisdom
  6. Charisma

Choose either one of these arrays, and assign them however you like to the six abilities.

  • 13, 13, 11, 11, 8, 8
  • 16, 13, 10, 8, 6, 5

Optional: Rolling ability scores.

If you wish, you can instead roll 3d6 seven times and use six of them to create a new array.

Choose a Class

There are many classes to choose from. Make sure you meet the requirements for that class. See Classes. If you are new to the game, we reccomnend fighter, thief, cleric, or wizard.

Unless you choose a Demihuman class, you are a human.

Note the following:

  1. Your HP (health points) is equal to the maximum value of your class's Health Die. (d4=4, d6=6, etc)
  2. The types of armor you can use.
  3. The types of weapon you can use.
  4. If you have 13 or more in your prime requisite, note a 10% xp bonus, or 15% if you have 13 or more in both.
  5. Your AV (attack value) is on the progression table. Its ussually 19.
  6. Your saving throws scores for physical and magic are on the progression table.
  7. The xp threshold for level 2.
  8. Class abilities and skills.

Items and money

You start with 3d6x10 cp in your bank account.

You have a base of ten item slots. One item per slot, heavy takes double, small items fit 5 per slot. Buy items LINK GOES HERE.

  1. Armor. Note the AC (armor class) score
  2. Weapon. Note the damage die and properties
  3. Holy symbol or thieves tools (if applicable)
  4. Other supplies such as rations, torches, bandages, sacks, rope, pole, spikes

Alignment

Choose whether you are Lawful, Neutral, or Chaotic. This impacts spellcasting and roleplay. Note that some classes have restrictions on which alignment they can be.

Beneath the surface of everyday life, two primeval forces mingle. Their servants see a cosmic harmony permieating the plane, or see only probibilities.

Magic

Magic affects creatures differently depending on alignment. Divine spellcastes need to be especially aware of their alignment; lawful casters may upset their diety by casting reversed spells, and vice versa. See LINKKKK for more details.

Alignment Language

Creatures automatically recognize a certain set of gestures, signs, and code words, pertaining to their alignment. This allows creatures of common alignment a measure of communication.

Creatures of a different alignment are likely to recognize an alignment language being spoken, but will not understand its content.

Revealing Alignment

Players must reveal their alignment to the dungeon master, but are not required to reveal to each other.

Role Playing Alignment

If the character acts too far out of their alignment, the referee may require them to change their alignment and suffer a penalty.

Changing Alignment

When changing alignment from Lawful or Chaotic, characters loose 10% of their xp.
