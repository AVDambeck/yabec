Hirelings

There are NPCs that help the party. These include trained animals, human hirelings which can be recruited to carry items and even fight in combat, and henchmen who level and grow like players. You may even be able to persuade monsters, or summon special creatures.

  • Use a note card or additional sheet of paper to keep track hirelings. You can use one card per each, or a large sheet with several.
  • Each character can be a boss to a finite number of hirelings determined by renown (as indicated by charisma).
  • Hirelings must have fewer hit dice than their boss
  • Hirelings must have their upkeep paid at the start of each session. If the session lasts more than 7 in game days, upkeep needs to be paid again each 7 days. Monsters with 10 or more loyalty do not need upkeep.
  • The boss must provide for rations while traveling, as well as for any new adventuring gear, weapons, or mounts.

Loyalty

Hirelings are not mindless slaves. If abused hirelings might desert, mutiny, betray, or slander their boss.

The starting loyalty score of hired hireling is indicated by charisma. Monsters start with 4 loyalty. Hirelings might change loyalty during clean up.

Hirelings with two or less loyalty will betray boss, and have a 4-in-6 chance of reducing their renown by one. Monsterous, and chaotic hirelings may cooperate only for a time, betraying their boss once the conditions are right.

Loyalty Check

Roll 2d6. The check succeeds if the result is equal to or less than the loyalty score.

Peril

Dangerous tasks are perilous. A hireling must succeed a loyalty check or refuse the task. Furthermore, if they take damage or come to any unexpected harm during the task, they loose one loyalty.

Example of peril include: fighting a risky and unnesscary battle, becoming lost in the dungeon, acting as bait, probing unknown objects.

Stats

The dungeon master has stats for animals and monsters. Hirelings have an AV of 19, 5 hit points, a physical save of 8, and a magic save of 5.

Henchmen

Henchmen are sidekicks which can gain XP and level up like players. There are two ways to gain a henchmen:

  1. If a hireling survives three sessions, they can become a level 0 henchman.
  2. A NPC can be persuaded to become a player's heanchmen through roleplay. The dungeon master will decide what level they start at.
  • Henchmen have 10 slots.
  • Henchmen have a d8 hit die, and calculate HP like players.
  • Henchmen go near-death and dismember like players.
  • When a boss gains xp, they can choose to direct upto 50% of that XP to their henchman.
  • Instead of upkeep, henchmen are paid with a portion of their boss's treasure. That portion is equal to two divided by their loyalty.
  • At 2nd level, they gain one ability. If their boss is a spellcaster, they can memorize one 1st level spell from their boss. Otherwise, they choose one fighter skill.
  • At 4th level, they gain a second ability. If their boss is a spellcaster, they can memorize one 2nd level spell from their boss. Otherwise they choose one advanced fighter skill.
