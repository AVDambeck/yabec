Overland Travel

Within one region of the world, travel can be handwaved, perhaps having a chance of random encounter. When traveling between regions, the dungeon master will prepare a map with events, and paths, as well as tables for survival events, monster encounters, and treasure.

Survival Points

The group begins with 7 travel points. They can never have more travel points than they start with. If they loose all travel points, everyone dismembers, and returns to the begining.

Travel points can be lost by failing survival events. They can be restored at rest sites.

Events

There are four general types of events, thought the dungeon master may further delinate these

  • Rest site
  • Survival event
  • Monster encounter
  • Location or mini dungeon

At the end of each event, the party must consume one ration, or loose a survival point.

All spells that end on a fatigue, burn, or shift also end at the end of an event.

Time of day

There are four times of day: dawn, noon, dusk, and night. Events may have different effects depending on the time of day.

Rest Site

At a rest site, the party chooses either to heal 1d6 and regain 1 survival point, or to roll on the treasure table. Below is a tiny example treasure table:

  1. Sword in the stone: a +1 sword is stuck in a stone. Removing it requires three consecutive successful strength checks, and one successful charisma check. Each character can only attempt once.
  2. Spider tree: a skeleton lies in a pool of water holding a shield of resistence 3. Above it lurks a 6HD adventure-eating spider. If night time, the ghost of the dead adventurer will also protect the shield.
  3. Blessed clearing: divine spell casters can pray in this clearing. It has a 6-in-6 chance to restore a 1st level spell, 3-in-6 to restore a 2nd level spell, and a 1-in-6 chance to restore a 3rd level spell.
  4. Mysterious stranger: a traveler approaches the party in search of healing from a potion of healing, cure wounds, or similar. If granted they will give a gift, depending on the time of day: dawn: a pearled silver pendent with the emblem of house Loimur worth 1d6 * 100 cp. noon: potion of d6 (1: bless 2: remove fear 3: fire resistence 4: cold resistence 5: poison 6: invisibility) dusk: random wizard spell scroll of 1st or 2nd level. night: random cursed magic ring. There is a 2-in-6 chance the stranger knows what item he has.

Survival Event

These are short challenges representing survival challanges. Below is a tiny example survival event table:

  1. Lost: loose one survival point. succed a wisdom check or loose a second.
  2. Shortcut: if you choose to take the shortcut, 3-in-6 chance to gain and 3-in-6 chance to loose a survival point.
  3. hungry wolves: feed them three rations or loose one survival point.
  4. Food poisoning: everyone makes constitution checks. If 50% or more fail, loose 1 survival point and a ration.
