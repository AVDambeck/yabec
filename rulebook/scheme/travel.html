Travel

Significant locations are represented by points. Routes between them are represented by paths.

Watch

Travel time is tracked in watches. There are three watches each day: morning, evening, and night.

Each watch, you can do one of the following:

  • Move from one point to another along a path.
  • Explore or town at your current point.
  • Forage desperetly. See starvation below.

At the end of each watch each character, and minion must eat a ration. If they fail to, they take 1d6 damage and become fatigued. Ignore this when in a civ point. To speed up play, you can opt to handle ratoins every 5 days instead.

Points

Points represent adventuring locations. There are three types of point:

  • Soft: Small feature which may be explored, e.g. an entrance to a cave.
  • Hard: Feature which must be explored e.g. a narrow pass in a mountain range.
  • Civ: Option to either town, clean up, or (so long as you aren't notorious) pass by.

Paths

There are three types:

  • Safe Path: No effect.
  • Risky Path: Chance of wandering monsters.
  • Toll Path: Toll in silver.

Starvation

If the party runs out of rations, they can forage: Any number of characters roll a d6. On a 4-5, they find one ration. On a 6, the find two. On a 1-4, they take 1d6 damage.

In dangerous areas, you might have to forage with disadvantage.
